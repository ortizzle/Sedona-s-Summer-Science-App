Physics Formula Reference
Units 1–4 · Keep this handy during practice
Motion
v = average velocity (m/s)  ·  Δx = displacement (m)  ·  Δt = time (s)
→ How fast something moved, and in which direction
a = acceleration (m/s²)  ·  vf = final velocity  ·  vi = initial velocity
→ How quickly velocity is changing (slowing down = negative a)
Forces (Newton's Laws)
Fnet = net force (N)  ·  m = mass (kg)  ·  a = acceleration (m/s²)
→ Newton's 2nd Law. More force or less mass = more acceleration
Every action force has an equal and opposite reaction force — on a different object
→ Newton's 3rd Law. Forces always come in pairs
Momentum & Impulse
p = momentum (kg·m/s)  ·  m = mass (kg)  ·  v = velocity (m/s)
→ A vector. Conserved in closed systems (nothing lost in a collision)
J = impulse (N·s)  ·  F = force (N)  ·  Δt = time (s)  ·  Δp = change in momentum
→ Why airbags work: longer Δt = smaller F for the same Δp
Gravitation
G = 6.67×10⁻¹¹ N·m²/kg²  ·  M, m = masses (kg)  ·  R = distance between centers (m)
→ Double R → force becomes ¼ as strong (Inverse Square Law)
m = mass (kg)  ·  g ≈ 9.8 m/s² near Earth's surface
→ Your weight. Changes on other planets; your mass never does
Energy & Work
KE = kinetic energy (J)  ·  m = mass (kg)  ·  v = speed (m/s)
→ Energy of motion. Speed squared — double the speed, 4× the energy
PE = potential energy (J)  ·  g ≈ 9.8 m/s²  ·  h = height (m)
→ Stored energy due to position. Higher up = more PE
W = work (J)  ·  F = force (N)  ·  d = displacement in direction of force (m)
→ Force must be in the same direction as motion to do work
